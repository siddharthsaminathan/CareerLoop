02 — Learning

What Emote taught me

01
Retention came from continuity, not novelty. Users returned when they felt the system understood prior context.
02
The product improved by studying behavior, not by adding random AI features. Every change earned its place in the workflow.
03
Latency and cost mattered because emotional products fail when they feel slow. Users notice sub-second delays in conversation.
04
The same principle applies to retail: every customer interaction should carry forward context across touchpoints.
05
People notice when a system remembers context correctly. That applies to a returning customer, a store associate, or a merchandising decision.
06
Shipping fast and measuring behavior beats shipping perfect and measuring opinions. Users showed us what mattered.

The important insight was not that people like AI. It was that people notice when a system remembers context correctly. That same principle applies to a returning customer, a store associate, a WhatsApp campaign, or a merchandising decision.

Conversational BI

My data engineering and dashboard work maps directly to Nicobar's vision of replacing static BI with a live conversational intelligence layer — merchandising, inventory, and campaign performance queried in plain language.
Design Workflows
